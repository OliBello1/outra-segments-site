Use case · Auto

3.2× match rate vs Meta lookalikes

How a tier-1 UK agency rebuilt their automotive lookalike model on Outra household audiences and 3.2×'d their addressable reach.

5 min read · February 2025

The problem

The agency had inherited an automotive client with strong first-party data — 24,000 verified buyers over 18 months — but a Meta lookalike audience that was plateauing at 1.4M reach with declining match quality.

The brief: prove that household-resolved lookalikes outperform Meta-native lookalikes on the same seed, the same campaign window, the same creative.

What we did

We uploaded the seed via clean-room to the Outra identity graph, resolved 21,300 of the 24,000 to UK households, scored every other UK household for behavioural similarity, and pushed the top decile to Meta as a CAPI-matched audience.

Same creative, same budget, two parallel cells. One on Meta lookalikes, one on Outra-resolved lookalikes.

The numbers

Match rate vs UK active households: 3.2× the Meta cell.

Cost per qualified lead: −22% on the Outra cell.

Frequency cap headroom: 1.8× before saturation.

What the planner said

"We've been doing lookalikes for a decade. This was the first time we ran a test where the result was unambiguous on the first day. We've now switched all auto clients to Outra-built audiences."
