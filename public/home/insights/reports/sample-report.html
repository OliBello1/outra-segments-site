Flagship report

The State of UK Household Data — 2025

Identity resolution, addressable TV and the cookieless transition are reshaping UK media. Our 24-page annual report covers what's actually changing and what to do about it.

Published April 2025 · 24 pages · PDF download

This is a sample report page. In production it would link to a downloadable PDF and embed key charts inline. The narrative below previews the executive summary.

Executive summary

UK addressability has consolidated decisively around household-level identity in 2025. Cookie-based audiences continue to decay as Safari, Firefox and Chrome restrictions bite; postcode-level proxies remain blunt; first-party data alone doesn't reach beyond a brand's own footprint. Household resolution — joining devices, signals and outcomes to a stable home identifier — is now the floor on which UK media planning sits.

Three things are different about 2025. First, addressable TV crossed 75% of UK homes. Second, retail media stopped being a Tesco-only conversation. Third, every major DSP now exposes a UK household identifier as a first-class targeting key.

What we measured

We resolved 30M+ UK households against the Outra identity graph, sampled activity across 25+ activation channels, and measured outcomes back through closed-loop attribution. The full report has the methodology in appendix A.

Top findings

1. Households move signals across 4.7 channels on average — not 1 or 2.

2. Postcode-level proxies misallocate 60% of mid-affluence campaigns.

3. Closed-loop CTV measurement is the single biggest 2025 opportunity.

What's next

The 2026 challenge isn't more signal. It's clean activation across every channel without breaking privacy posture. Outra's view: clean-rooms are necessary but not sufficient. Household identifiers, governed at the source, are what unlock the next 10x.
